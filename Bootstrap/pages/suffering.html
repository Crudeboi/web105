"The Suffering" is a third-person shooter video game set in a maximum-security prison. Players take on the role of Torque, a man sentenced to death for a crime he may or may not have committed. The game's story is filled with horror and psychological elements. As Torque, you must navigate the prison while facing horrifying creatures that represent the twisted past of the prison's inmates. These monstrous enemies are the result of supernatural forces and the suffering that has taken place within the prison's walls. The game offers a choice-based morality system, allowing players to make decisions that impact the story's outcome. As Torque, you can choose to help other inmates or be more self-centered, affecting your character's transformation. "The Suffering" is known for its dark and eerie atmosphere, with terrifying monsters and a sense of impending doom. The game focuses on combat, with a variety of weapons and abilities to take on the nightmarish creatures. Throughout the game, you'll uncover the prison's dark secrets and Torque's own past. The game blends horror and action, creating a suspenseful and chilling experience that keeps players on the edge of their seats. Overall, "The Suffering" offers a tense and scary adventure as players must confront their own demons while battling the monstrous horrors lurking within the prison, making it a memorable and unsettling gaming experience.

The suffering will always hold a special place in my heart. I never bought the game. I didn't have $59.99 as a child. So my dad would rent it for me from the local video store. When those were a thing at least. I bought the game guide for "The Suffering" and it was the first timele I ever did for any game. For those who don't know, game guides let you get every little bit from the game. From hidden amo to secret locations you probably wouldn't have been able to find on your own. I would stay up late making sure I squeezed every single bit of this game. It was the first linear story I ever finished. I played many games before this, but "The Suffering" solidified my interest in gaming forever.

4.7/5.0
